It is a 5.5-year bachelor’s degree course The main subjects in this course are History of Ayurveda, Kriya Sharira (Physiology), Pharmaceuticals of ayurveda, Pathology, Medical Ethics, Health Regulations etc.

Job opputunities after this course:



Just like other forms of medicine, Ayurvedic doctors examine the patients and document their medical history. The diagnosis involves consideration of the entire history of the patient. For this a comprehensive knowledge of the physical, physiological and mental state of the patient is gathered and systematically recorded.

Details about the patient’s temperament, habits, diet, external living conditions and details about the disease itself are recorded. After diagnosis, the doctor prescribes the appropriate medication and other remedial measures. An important part of the job is to explain the dosage of medicines and effect of the therapy to the patient.

Healthcare is a very vast field and has endless number of employment opportunities: One can find employment in:

1) General and Multi specialty hospitals - both government hospitals and corporate hospitals, Polyclinics and specialty clinics, Nursing homes, Industrial hospitals generally attached to large factories

2) Medical Services of the Army, Navy and Air Force under the Ministry of Defense.

3) Establish your own practice.

4) Medical Colleges, which offer courses like MBBS, MD, MS, DM, M.Ch., etc.

5) Health care centres run by governments and private organizations in various district headquarters, small towns and even in villages.

6) Pharmaceutical companies in their sales support functions, medical documentation function, or as an advisor in the Medical Advisory Board after you have 4-5 years of experience.

7) Opportunities also lie in the realms of forensic science. You could be at the forefront of solving great mysteries by merely identifying the teeth composition or called upon for autopsies
.
8) Dental doctors could work in companies producing toothpastes and toothbrushes. From suggesting alternative formulae for pastes and powders, to upgrading the existing products.

9) The field of nutrition and dietetics has a wide range of applications: Health Care, Social Welfare, Institutional Catering, Food Services, Research and Development, Education and Health clubs
